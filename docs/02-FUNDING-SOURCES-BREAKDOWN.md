# PNG National e-Government Procurement System
## Funding Sources & Financial Structure
### Document Version: 1.0 | Date: January 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The PNG National e-Government Procurement System requires a total investment of approximately **USD $2,990,000** for implementation over 24 months, with annual operating costs of approximately **USD $500,000** thereafter.

This document outlines potential funding sources, contribution structures, and financial mechanisms to support the project.

| Operating Account (PGK) | Local currency expenses | K 500,000 | Monthly |

### 7.3 Disbursement Procedures

**For Donor Funds:**
1. Prepare withdrawal application with supporting documents
2. Submit to Development Partner
3. Review and approval (5-10 working days)
4. Transfer to Designated Account
5. Payment to contractors/vendors

**For Loan Funds:**
1. Direct Payment: Bank pays vendor directly
2. Reimbursement: Bank reimburses pre-financed expenditure
3. Special Commitment: Bank pays against Letter of Credit

**For Government Funds:**
1. Quarterly cash flow forecast
2. Treasury warrant request
3. Budget release
4. Transfer to Project Account

## 8. Co-Financing Arrangements

### 8.1 Parallel Financing

Each funder finances specific components independently:

| Funder | Components | Procurement |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Government | Infrastructure, Staff, Venues | PNG Procurement Act |
| DFAT | Core Development, TA | Commonwealth Procurement |
| EU | OCDS, Transparency | EU PRAG |
| World Bank | Hardware, Consulting | WB Procurement Regs |

### 8.2 Pooled Financing (Alternative)

All funds pooled into single account with joint oversight:

**Advantages:**
- Simplified financial management
- Single reporting format
- Reduced transaction costs

**Challenges:**
- Different audit requirements
- Visibility/branding issues
- Procurement harmonization

**Recommendation:** Parallel financing with harmonized reporting
